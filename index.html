Game Concept

Concept Overview: is an RTS that immerses players in the strategic and tactical challenges of World War I. The game emphasizes a balance between economy, strategy and real time action.

Inspiration: Gameplay is heavily inspired by Age of Empires II and Knights of Honour. The artistic and thematic style is heavily inspired by the Grimdark aesthetic frequently found in Warhammer 40,000.

Core Gameplay: Players assume the roles of historical factions, orchestrating large-scale military operations on the battlegrounds of Europe. The game features large strategic decisions like research, production and moving regiments, and close up fast tactical play.

Unique Selling Points:

  • Fresh Experience: WWI is a very underrepresented theatre in gaming, only 1 in every 36 RTS games are about WWI (approx). This gives players a much wider range of new units, tactics and experiences to explore than many RTS games offer. This provides a young game studio a blank canvas to work with.
  • Strategic Depth: A lot of moving parts that are packaged neatly into 2 distinct map scales and 4 distinct tactical stages. Both small decisions and large decisions can have meaningful impact on the outcome.
  • Olde World Charm, Modern Mechanical Monstrosities: The WWI period intersected pre-industrial equipment and tactics with the horrors of death delivered at a new industrial scale. This game will capture the blend of old and new in a way not seen before. A potent cocktail of cavalry charges and mustard gas, Napoleonic era formations and analogue targeting computers. Unique!

Gameplay Mechanics

The game loop is divided between the macro and micro management of 2 maps and 4 General Orders that run in real time. The 2 maps cover different scales of the battlefield, a large view of whole regions or a granular view of specific battlefields, zooming with the mouse roller will switch scales. The stages of General Orders are a more abstract concept but are basically like "ages" in Age of Empires, levels of advancement that have requirements to reach and provide new opportunities at each stage. General Orders cannot be undone once called up. There is no limitation on where anything can be built or moved but there are sensible places to build and move. Like building barracks in urban areas or steel foundries close to iron deposits is a good idea. But there might be good reasons to build or move near or in enemy areas, like field hospitals or incursions behind enemy lines. Here is a more detailed description of the game loops sections:

Maps


Macro Scale: Strategic Map

This is high level map view covers whole regions for macro management. A player uses this map view to manage production, research and moving entire armies. At this scale, the player handles economy like workers, resource management, production of units and logistics (roads, rail, shipping). The strategic map view will handle nearly all the economy game elements with very few military elements, just strategic bombing.

Micro Scale: Tactical Map

This detailed map view covers specific battlefields for micro management. The player has tight, responsive control over units. The player will command individual units to collect supplies, dig (gun pits, trenches, dugouts, tunnels), build (wire, lay mines, lookout posts, machine gun nests), move or attack. Using hotkeys will be crucial. Most of the military game elements are handled at this scale with very few economy elements handled here, just wood chopping for lumber. There is an additional layer to this map which is that players can engage in tunnel warfare by digging through 3 subterranean layers under the battlefield.

General Orders

The stages of the game are called "General Orders". Each order affects economic and military elements. Although the button to call up the next General Order is accessed through the capital city located on the Strategic Map, the order will affect all areas of the game. General Orders are as follows:

1: Mobilisation

Focus on rapidly gathering essential resources and swiftly advancing to the next General Order. Efficient resource management and early strategic planning are key.

2: Fortification

Prioritize the construction of critical defence structures and expand your military forces. Launch probing attacks to gauge enemy strength, positions and attempt to hamper their rate of expansion.

3: Assault

Launch aggressive attacks to weaken and dislodge enemy forces. Simultaneously, continue expanding your military and economy, advance in technology research and secure strategically advantageous positions on the battlefield.

4: Hold the Line

The battle reaches its crescendo with full-scale onslaughts on multiple fronts. Finalise research on remaining technologies to ensure your units have superior capabilities. This is the last desperate, all out struggle to win the game.

To call up the next General Order the player needs to pay certain resources, have certain buildings and unlock certain technologies. The requirement for the next General Order increases but so do the rewards of each General Order, access to new and better units and buildings, accesses to new and better upgrades on existing units and buildings. Calling up the next General Order before your opponent will often put a player at an advantage but there may be some situations/strategies where spending resources on increasing economy or military is more advantageous than calling up the next General Order.

Story and Setting

Story mode will be a feature to implement if there is time, single player skirmish gameplay will be the primary focus. Campaign and multiplayer will be added if time allows.

The storyline serves as a backdrop to the gameplay, enriching the player's experience without overpowering it. Emphasis is placed on robust game mechanics and strategic depth, ensuring engaging gameplay. While the story offers context and emotional depth, it is the strategic and tactical choices that drive the game, allowing players to immerse themselves in the challenges of command during The Great War.

Many war games are either amoral or throw morality out of the window in favour of propaganda tropes. The storyline of this game will address morality directly and place the blame squarely on the shoulders of the politicians who dragged their nations into yet more pointless wars. The story will not be propagandistic but it will also not shy away from the blood guilt incurred by the leaders of that time. Nations themselves will not be painted as good guys or bad guys in the campaign. Real historic reference material will be used as inspiration for the story.

The game should not be pinned down by historical accuracy but use history as inspiration, heavy artistic licence can be used if needed to improve the gaming experience. The player should have maximum agency to impact the course of the game, therefore there must be scope for an outcome that differs from reality.

If you fancy an entertaining overview of the narrative style, check this video about The Seige of Vraks in Warhammer 40,000.

Again, story mode will only be built if time allows, it's not the primary focus, gameplay is. This gives plenty of space for DLCs.

Art Style

The art style of can be summed up in a word quite easily: Grimdark atmosphere, dieselpunk detail. It strikes a balance between historical accuracy and fun aesthetics. While maintaining accurate proportions of units, buildings, and scenery, the details are rendered in a stylised, somewhat cartoonish quality. This is complemented by thematic shades, diverging from strict realism to enhance the game's atmosphere. The skies and horizons are particularly dramatic, styled with dark, ominous features that create impactful backdrops, evoking the intense emotions of war. This stylistic choice aims to make the game visually appealing and engaging while preserving gravitas. For visual inspiration and a better understanding of this artistic direction, check out this video showcasing the Death Guard legion, which exemplifies a similar blend of realism and stylisation. When polishing the game we should add some exciting lighting and screen shake effects on explosions and eerie effects of gas clouds creeping over battlefields. A day/night/season cycle and weather should be a visual representation of certain game mechanics like visibility, gas weapons and terrain movement effects (hard or soft mud based on season and weather).

Since the primary focus of is on mechanical gameplay, the development of the art style will be a subsequent phase. The initial stages of the project will concentrate on solidifying the core gameplay mechanics, ensuring they are engaging, functional, and well-tuned. Once the gameplay foundation is strong the art development will commence. This approach will ensure that the visual elements complement and enhance the established mechanics, rather than overpower them. Because there will be many units onscreen at any one time, the computational load of visuals will be handled economically to ensure performance doesn't tank.

Once the mechanical aspects of the prototype are well underway, digital artists/3D Modellers will be sought to handle game art. Their role will be to create the feel of game aesthetics, create high-poly models and convert to sprite sheets, and create animations for presenting the demo to players and investors.

Sound and Music

Sound Design

  • Sound Engineer: Similar to handling art during the demo development phase, a sound engineer will be sought to contribute his expertise to the project after the core game mechanics are solidly in place. His role will involve crafting a compelling and immersive audio landscape that enhances gameplay and narrative. This includes creating and sourcing sound effects, ambient sounds, and potentially working on the game's musical score. The sound engineer is expected to bring his own specialized tools and resources to the project, and collaborate closely with the development team to ensure the audio aligns with the game's artistic vision and gameplay dynamics.
  • Prioritization of Sound Effects: During development, the highest priority is given to effects that directly enhance gameplay mechanics. This approach ensures that sounds crucial for player feedback, incoming artillery fire or your units carrying out orders, are developed first to aid in strategic decision-making and improve the overall gaming experience.
  • Event Driven Sound Effects: Tailored audio cues that give players vital information about in-game events, enhancing gameplay awareness and immersion through responsive, contextual sounds. For example, a distant siren indicating an impending attack, enhancing the player's situational awareness.
  • Context-Sensitive Ambient Sounds: Trigger specific ambient sound effects based on the player's location and the events happening around them. For example, near a battlefield, the sounds of distant artillery fire could be heard, whereas in a base camp, the focus might shift to the chatter of soldiers and the clanking of equipment.
  • Multilingual Voice Acting for Factions: Each faction's voice acting is in its national language, enhancing authenticity and appealing to players' cultural identity, particularly in non-English speaking countries. This approach heightens immersion and personal connection to the game.

Music

  • Event Driven Background Tracks: Employ more upbeat music during the final General Order of Hold The Line.
  • Royalty-Free Music: Use royalty-free music tracks available online. Websites like Incompetech or Free Music Archive offer a variety of tracks that can be used for free or with attribution.
  • Minimalist Approach: Minimalist approach to music. Sometimes, less is more – a few well-chosen tracks can be effective for setting the mood.
  • Public Domain Historical Music: Research and incorporate music from the era that is now in the public domain. Early 20th-century compositions might be available for use without licensing issues.

Environment Design

The design of environments in will lean on the landscapes and settings of World War I but prioritising the game's strategic and tactical gameplay over realism.

  • Historically Inspired Battlefields: Levels are based on real World War I battlefields, featuring authentic terrains like trenches and war-torn villages.
  • Interactive Environments: Environments are interactive, providing tactical advantages and depth to gameplay. For example, being on a hill increases view and weapon range.
  • Dynamic Weather and Time: Changing weather conditions and time-of-day cycles affect movement and visibility, adding strategic elements.
  • Destructible Elements: Certain scenery features are destructible, allowing dynamic battlefield changes.
  • Varied Scales and Perspectives: Different map scales offer a deeper gameplay experiences, from the large macro strategic map view, the detailed micro tactical map and an addiitonal scroll level to handle underground tunnel warfare.
  • Atmospheric Detailing: Small details like spent shell casings and makeshift encampments enhance the immersive atmosphere.

User Interface

The UI of will closely follow the established RTS UI paradigm for familiarity and ease of adoption by new players. Drawing inspiration from the efficient and user-friendly Age of Empires II UI system, we will replicate its effectiveness without unnecessary innovation. However, the aesthetic design will be tailored to fit the setting of our game, enhancing the visual appeal and overall gaming experience.

  • Mouse:
    • Cursor to Window Edge: Moving the cursor to the edge of the game window scrolls the view around the map, allowing players to navigate different areas seamlessly.
    • Left Button:
      • Select Units/Buildings: A single left-click selects a unit or building, displaying its options and information.
      • Drag Selection: Clicking and dragging selects multiple units, enabling group commands and management.
    • Right Button:
      • Issue Commands: A right-click directs selected units to move, attack, gather resources, or interact with the environment.
    • Scroll Wheel:
      • Zoom In/Out: Scrolling up or down zooms the camera in or out, changing perspective between the macro strategic map, the micro tactical map or underground tunnels and dugouts.
  • Keyboard:
    • [1-0] keys will be reserved for Unit Assignment.
    • [QWERT] keys will be reserved for Actions like "construction" or "attack".
    • [ASDFG] keys will be reserved for Stances like "aggressive", "defensive", "stand ground", etc.
    • [ZXCVB] keys will be reserved for Formations like "line", "tight box", "large box", etc.
    • General hotkeys Will be available for buildings and "jumping to" a view, like a particular part of a particular battlefield.

    The key functions will be nested, for example, selecting a worker or soldier will open all the options since they can perform all options, but a building cannot move, therefore the Formation options will not open when a building is selected. Or an artillery gun cannot build anything, so the Construction Action options will not open when an artillery gun is selected.

    The [QWERTASDFZXCV] keys and the number row have been strategically chosen for their ergonomic positioning, making them ideally suited for rapid and efficient hotkey-based gameplay. Their physical placement on the keyboard will align seamlessly with the 5x3 grid layout of the game's options menu, facilitating intuitive and swift navigation (refer to the accompanying image for visual layout).

  • On Screen Menu:

    The on-screen control menu is designed for accessibility, with options selectable via left mouse clicks and closable through right mouse clicks. While clicking on-screen buttons is conventional in RTS games and will be included for familiarity, it's acknowledged as less efficient for controlling gameplay. Therefore, the game will emphasize the use of hotkeys, guiding players to more efficient control methods while still providing the standard point-and-click interface.

  • Hotkey Scoping:

    Given the large number of units to manage, the standard 10 hotkeys for units may prove insufficient for fast and engaging micromanagement. To address this, while on the macro strategic map, direct unit control is not available, hotkeys focusing instead on broader strategic control like selecting cities, steel foundries or gun factories. Players can quickly switch to a specific micro tactical map using a dedicated hotkey [1-0]. In this tactical view, unit hotkeys become active but are localized to the currently viewed battlefield. This design ensures that each unit has a unique hotkey assignment within its respective tactical map, allowing for efficient management of numerous units without conflicts. Additionally, battlefield locations and hotkey assignments are stored as member properties of unit objects for seamless gameplay.

Technology

This game will be programmed at a low level using C++ and a few complementary libraries. No game engine will be used. The reason is it's a project designed to increase programming skills, team management and enterprise skills. Sticking to raw code provides the greatest learning environment at the cost of time. Skills gained on this project are directly applicable to future projects that need low level problem-solving skills making a practice ground.

RTS Genre Trend

Rekindled: Analytics of a Genre Finding Renewed Fire

The real-time strategy (RTS) genre, once a titan of the gaming world, has undergone a fascinating transformation. While its market share and mainstream presence have undoubtedly shrunk compared to FPS and MOBA juggernauts, recent data hints at a subtle but promising resurgence. Let's delve into the numbers to paint a clearer picture.

Market Trends:
  • Declined, but now Stabilising: According to Newzoo, the RTS market saw a steady decline from 2012 to 2018, falling from 5% of the global PC market to just 1.5%. However, since then, it has stabilized, holding steady at around 1.4% in 2022. This suggests a core, dedicated audience that remains engaged.
  • Indie Spark: While AAA RTS releases have become rarer, the indie scene has stepped up. According to GameDev.net, the number of new RTS titles released annually has remained constant around 100-150 since 2018. This steady stream of innovation, often focusing on tighter mechanics and streamlined controls, is attracting new players and revitalizing the community.
Competitive Scene:
  • Esports: While not dominating the esports landscape as in its heydays, titles like StarCraft II and Age of Empires IV maintain vibrant competitive communities. Smaller titles like Iron Harvest are also building their competitive scenes, offering opportunities for dedicated RTS players.
  • Streaming: Twitch viewership for RTS games, while not in the top tiers, shows promise. According to StreamElements, StarCraft II consistently pulls in around 30,000 viewers, with Age of Empires IV and smaller titles like They Are Billions also garnering healthy audiences. This indicates continued interest and engagement from the core community.
Challenges and Potential:
  • Steep Learning Curve: RTS games remain notoriously challenging to master, with complex mechanics and demanding multitasking. This high barrier to entry can deter new players, limiting the genre's growth potential.
  • Console Conundrum: The traditional RTS control scheme, heavily reliant on mouse and keyboard, doesn't translate well to consoles, a massive gaming market segment. Innovative control solutions or genre-specific console games are crucial for broader reach.
Looking Ahead:

The RTS genre isn't experiencing a full-blown renaissance, but it's certainly far from dead. Dedicated developers are injecting fresh ideas, a passionate community keeps the flame alive, and data suggests a stable, engaged player base. Continued accessibility improvements, innovative control schemes, and captivating new approaches to classic mechanics hold the key to rekindling the RTS fire and attracting a wider audience. While navigating the challenges, the genre has the potential to carve out a unique and thriving space in the modern gaming landscape, proving that strategic depth and real-time empire building still hold a powerful allure for players seeking a cerebral and rewarding gaming experience.

Target Audience

The target audience for our RTS is specifically chosen and will not be left to chance. Our primary focus will be on fans who cherish the classic RTS mechanics found in games like Command & Conquer and Age of Empires, and who also have a passion for the Grimdark aesthetic of the Warhammer 40,000 universe. This audience is known for their appreciation of:

  • Strategic Complexity: They enjoy games that require thoughtful planning, resource management, and tactical decision-making. This audience appreciates the challenge of balancing long-term strategy with immediate tactical choices.
  • Nostalgia and Franchise Loyalty: Many players of classic RTS games have a sense of nostalgia and loyalty towards these franchises. They often seek experiences that remind them of the classic gameplay but also incorporate modern enhancements.
  • Grimdark Theme: Fans of the Warhammer 40,000 universe are drawn to its dark, gritty, and complex setting. Incorporating this aesthetic can attract players who are looking for an RTS experience that combines classic gameplay with a rich, immersive world of towering industrial might and old world stylisation.
  • Competitive and Cooperative Play: Emulating the competitive spirit of classic RTS games and the cooperative aspects of Warhammer 40,000 gameplay, we aim to attract players who enjoy both solo and multiplayer experiences.
  • Modding and Community Involvement: The ability to modify games and be part of a vibrant community is a significant draw. Many players of classic RTS titles are active in forums, modding communities, and online groups dedicated to these games.

In terms of growth potential, there's an opportunity to attract younger gamers who may not have experienced these classic titles. A new game opens the door to new ledgends in the eSports scene which is rich with younger players, their enthusiasm will help recruit a new generation of RTS fans. Additionally, by capturing the essence of nostalgia inherent in retro and classic gaming, we can tap into the fond memories and deep affections held by players for these iconic titles, rekindling their passion and inviting them to revisit the evolved world of RTS gaming through our offering.

Monetisation

Until we have a working demo, our primary focus should not be on monetisation. Instead, our efforts should be dedicated to developing a compelling, high-quality product. However, as part of our broader marketing strategy, we should consider creating a crowdfunding campaign. This approach not only helps in raising funds but more specifically in building a community of early supporters and receiving valuable feedback. Post-demo completion, we can explore monetisation strategies such as direct sales on Steam, cosmetic item microtransactions, and participation in the Steam Trading Cards system. Using Steam Keys will allow us to pay 0% fees to Steam while keeping the functionality the platform provides.

Localising sales prices, a built-in functionality when releasing games on Steam, is vital for accessing markets with high turnover but less disposable income per capita. A $20 price tag might feel like a steal to an American gamer, but it could easily put off the large gaming communities in Brazil, South Korea or Vietnam. Steam's built-in localisation functionality removes this barrier, allowing us to tailor prices to each nation. This strategic approach opens doors to global markets, makes the game significantly more accessible, and ensures players perceive our pricing as offering excellent value relative to their own economic environment.

While mapping out a monetisation strategy is crucial for long-term sustainability, it should not take precedence over crafting a truly exceptional game, especially at this early phase. Our primary focus should be building a product that resonates deeply with our fans and potential investors. A game brimming with passion and engaging gameplay will naturally attract support and revenue in its own right. Remember, a captivating experience speaks volumes, paving the way for sustainable monetisation built on a foundation of genuine player love and enjoyment.

Advertising

Advertising plays a pivotal role in the success of both games and studios. Notably, some AAA studios allocate as much as 50% of their multi-million dollar budget to marketing efforts, highlighting the significance of effective promotion. In line with this industry insight, we will leverage every available free advertising resource, with a readiness to allocate funds as they become accessible. Our commitment to advertising remains steadfast, regardless of our current funding status. Once we have sufficient gameplay content, we will seek to welcome a new team member dedicated to advertising and promotion. Here is the outline of approach to advertising, both before and after securing funding:

Before Funding

Social media platforms like Facebook, VK, and Twitch are ideal for posting regular updates, sharing behind-the-scenes glimpses, and actively engaging with our audience. Additionally, tapping into localised gaming communities can significantly amplify our reach. Focusing on regional markets, which are often less saturated, presents an opportunity for our game to stand out. By tailoring content and interactions to these specific audiences, we can establish a unique presence in areas where competition may be less intense, potentially capturing the attention of an engaged and dedicated user base that larger markets may overlook. This granular, localised approach can be a key strategy in differentiating our game in a challenging global marketplace.

As part of our localisation strategy, partnering with smaller, local content creators focused on indie games can be highly impactful. Offering them early access not only garners potential reviews but also exposes our game to their region-specific audiences. This approach fosters grassroots-level marketing, the valuable "human touch", creating authentic engagement within local gaming communities. Such collaborations can effectively resonate with players on a more personal and cultural level, enhancing the visibility and appeal of our game in an organic way.

Utilising Discord and Reddit, we will aim to build a strong community around our game. A Discord server offers a space for real-time interaction and updates, fostering a sense of belonging among players. Meanwhile, a dedicated subreddit allows for deeper discussions and feedback, contributing to a community-driven growth. These platforms are integral in creating an engaged and interactive player base.

Last but definitely not least, we will use analytics to refine our advertising approach. By examining community engagement and behaviour patterns, we can identify the most effective channels and content types for our marketing. This data-driven strategy allows us to concentrate our efforts where they yield the best results, maximising the impact of our advertising in terms of both time and resources. Analytics provide us with the insights necessary to tailor our message to the right audience at the right time, ensuring our promotional activities are both efficient and effective.

After Funding

If/when funding is secured, we can expand and improve our advertising toolbox. This increased budget will allow us to explore paid advertising options, such as targeted social media ads, collaborations with high-profile influencers, and potentially even display advertising on gaming websites. Enhanced funding also opens up opportunities for more creative and widespread campaigns. Moreover, the valuable analytics data collected during our early stages of advertising will be instrumental in this phase. This data will guide us in efficiently allocating our advertising funds, ensuring we invest in the most effective channels and strategies that have shown promising engagement and conversion rates, thus making the best use of our resources.

Creating a comprehensive press-kit is another crucial step. This kit will include high-quality game screenshots, engaging trailers, a well-crafted press release, and key information about our game. It's designed to make it easy for game journalists and bloggers to cover our story, thereby increasing our media presence.

Presenting our work at gaming conventions should be high priority. These events will be a great opportunity to showcase the game, infuse the advertising campaign with our personal enthusiasm, gain valuable feedback, and build hype directly with the gaming community. Such personal interactions can significantly boost the game's visibility and appeal. When selecting which events to attend, we'll strictly adhere to the analytic data collected from our early advertising efforts. This granular, localised approach to advertising might lead us to some exotic locations, but that's perfectly fine. Our aim is to follow the data, ensuring we target events that are most likely to attract our intended audience and provide the best opportunities for meaningful engagement, thereby maximizing the impact of our presence at the gaming event.

Budget Estimation

Working with a zero budget initially, our financial strategy is straightforward: We must brutally bootstrap everything! However, once the game reaches its early access stage, we plan to initiate a crowdfunding campaign. The aim of this campaign is twofold: to secure funds for a Steam launch and marketing efforts, but more importantly, to serve as a form of free advertising to build awareness and cultivate a fanbase ahead of the game's release.
