Game Concept

Concept Overview: is an RTS that immerses players in the strategic and tactical challenges of World War I. The game emphasizes a balance between economy, strategy and real time action.

Inspiration: Gameplay is heavily inspired by Age of Empires II and Kingdom Kights. The artistic and thematic style is heaviliy inspried by the Death Korps of Krieg and Death Guard Legion of Warhammer 40,000.

Core Gameplay: Players assume the roles of historical factions, orchestrating large-scale military operations on the battlegrounds of Europe. The game features large strategic decisions like research, production and moving regiments, and close up fast tactial play.

Unique Selling Points:

  • Fresh Experience: WWI is a very under represented theatre in gaming, only 1 in every 36 RTS games are about WWI (aprox). This gives players a much wider range of new units, tactics and experiences to explore than many RTS games offer. This provides a new game studio a blank canvas to work with.
  • Strategic Depth: A lot of moving parts that are packaged neatly into 2 distinct map scales and 4 distinct tactical stages. Both small decisions and large decisions can have meaninful impact the outcome.
  • Olde World Charm, Modern Mechanical Monstrosities: WWI intersceted pre industrial equipment and tactics with the horrors of death delivered at the industrial scale. This game will capture the blend of old and new in a way not seen before. A potent cocktail of cavalry charges and mustard gas, Napoleonic era formations and analog targeting computers. Unique!

Gameplay Mechanics

The game loop is divided between the macro and micro management of 2 maps and 4 General Orders that run in real time. The 2 maps cover different scales of the battlefield, a large view of whole regions or a granular view of specific battlefields, zooming with the mouse roller will switch scales. The stages of Genreal Orders are a more abstract concept but are basically like "ages" in Age of Empires, levels of advancement that have requirments to reach and provide new opertunities at each stage. General Orders cannot be undone once called up. There is no limitation on where anything can be built or moved but there are sensible places build and move. Like building barracks in urban areas or steel foundries close to iron deposits is a good idea. But there might be good reasons to build or move near or in enemy areas, like field hospitals or incusions behind enemy lines. Here is a more detailed description of the game loops sections:

Maps


Macro Scale: Strategic Map

This is high level map view covers whole regions for macro management. A player uses this map view to manage production, research and moving entire armies. At this scale the player handles economy like workers, resource management, prodcution of units and logistics (roads, rail, shipping). The strategic map view will handle nearly all the econmy game elements with very few military elements, just strategic bombing.

Micro Scale: Tactical Map

This detailed map view covers specific battlefields for micro management. The player has tight, responsive control over units. The player will command individual units to collect supplies, dig (gun pits, trenches, dugouts, tunnels), build (wire, lay mines, lookout posts, machine gun nests), move or attack. Using hotkeys will be crucial. Most of the military game elements are handled at this scale with very few economy elements handled here, just wood chopping for lumber. There is an additional layer to this map which is that players can engage in tunnel warfare by digging through 3 subterrainian layers under the battlefield.

General Orders

The stages in game are called "General Orders". Each order affects economic and military elements. Although the button to call up the next General Order is accessed through the capital city located on the Strategic Map, the order will affect all areas of the game. General Orders are as follows:

Mobilisation

Focus on rapidly gathering essential resources and swiftly advancing to the next General Order. Efficient resource management and early strategic planning are key.

Fortification

Prioritize the construction of critical defense structures and expand your military forces. Launch probing attacks to gauge enemy strength, positions and attempt to hamper their rate of expansion.

Assault

Launch aggressive attacks to weaken and dislodge enemy forces. Simultaneously, continue expanding your military and economy, advance in technology research and secure strategically advantageous positions on the battlefield.

Hold the Line

Implement a full-scale offensive to overwhelm the enemy. Finalize research on remaining technologies to ensure your units have superior capabilities. Focus on leveraging your maximum military potential for a decisive victory.

To call up the next General Order the player needs to pay certain resources, have certain buildings and unlock certain technologies. The requirment for the next General Order increases but so do the rewards of each General Order, access to new and better units and buildings, accesss to new and better upgrades on existing units and buildings. Calling up the next General Order before your opponent will often put a player at an advantage but there may be some situations/strategies where spending resources on increasing economy or military is more advantageous than calling up the next General Order.

Story and Setting

Story mode will be a feature to implement if there is time, single player skirmish gameplay will be the primary focus. Campaign and multiplayer will be added if time allows.

The storyline serves as a backdrop to the gameplay, enriching the player's experience without overpowering it. Emphasis is placed on robust game mechanics and strategic depth, ensuring engaging gameplay. While the story offers context and emotional depth, it is the strategic and tactical choices that drive the game, allowing players to immerse themselves in the challenges of command during The Great War.

Many war games are either amoral or throw morality out of the window in favor of propaganda tropes. The storyline of this game will address morality directly and place the blame squarley on the shoulders of the politicians who dragged their nations into yet more pointless wars. The story will not be propegandistic but it will also not shy away from the blood guilt incurred by the leaders of that time. Nations themselves will not be painted as good guys or bad guys in the campaign. Real historic reference material will be used as inspiration for the story.

The game should not be pinned down by historical accuracy but use history as inspiration, heavy artistic licence can be used if needed to improve the gaming experience. The player should have maximum agency to impact the course of the game, therefore there must be scope for an outcome that differs from reality.

If you fancy an entertaining overview of the narrative style, check this video about The Seige of Vraks in Warhammer 40,000.

Again, story mode will only be built if time allows, it's not the primary focus, gameplay is. This gives plenty of space for DLCs.

Art Style

The art style of strikes a balance between historical accuracy and approachable aesthetics. While maintaining accurate proportions of units, buildings, and scenery, the details are rendered in a stylized, somewhat cartoonish quality. This is complemented by thematic shades, diverging from strict realism to enhance the game's atmosphere. The skies and horizons are particularly dramatic, styled with dark, ominous features that create impactful backdrops, evoking the intense emotions of war. This stylistic choice aims to make the game visually appealing and engaging while preserving the gravity of the historical context. For visual inspiration and a better understanding of this artistic direction, check out this video showcasing the Death Guard legion, which exemplifies a similar blend of realism and stylisation. There should be some exciting lighting and screen shake effects on explosions and eerie effects of gas clouds creeping over battlefields. A day/night/season cycle and weather should be a visual representation of certain game mechanics like visability, gas weapons and terrain movement effects (hard or soft mud).

Since the primary focus of is on mechanical gameplay, the development of the art style will be a subsequent phase. The initial stages of the project will concentrate on solidifying the core gameplay mechanics, ensuring they are engaging, functional, and well-tuned. Once the gameplay foundation is strong the art development will commence. This approach will ensure that the visual elements complement and enhance the established mechanics rather than overpower them. Because there will be many units onscreen at any one time the computational load of visuals will be handled economically to ensure performance doesn't tank.

Technology

This game will be programmed at a low level using C++ and a few complementary libraries. No game engine will be used. The reason is it's a project designed to increase programming skills, team management and enterprise skills. Sticking to raw code provides the greatest learning environment at the cost of time. Skills gained on this project are directly applicable to future projects that need low level problem solving skills making a practice ground.
